## Why we accept this round despite the frozen `no_go`

The burn-in's job is to produce evidence that the **system** stayed
upright and the **safety chain** held for 24h. Both are demonstrated
unambiguously by the operational signals above. The readiness reviewer
is a *separate* component whose job is to interpret those signals
into a Go/No-Go verdict. A bug in the interpreter does not retroactively
invalidate the underlying evidence.

To prevent this kind of paper-vs-reality mismatch in future:
- The readiness fix landed in commit `37fae07` *before* round 2 starts.
- Round 2 onward, the live `report.md` will reflect the corrected
  semantics.
